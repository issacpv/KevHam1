Essay Williamsburg 34 wks
The Bagel Shop on Bedford That Nobody Talks About Anymore
There's a place on Bedford that's been there since before the coffee shops, before the galleries, before the entire transformation of this neighborhood into what it is now. I walked past it yesterday and noticed the sign is finally faded past legibility.

It was a bagel shop. It's still technically open — the lights are on most mornings — but the regulars who used to line up at 6am have been gone for three years. What's left is a kind of persistence that I find more honest than anything that replaced it.

I don't know if that's symbolic or if I'm just projecting.

Essay Jackson Heights 9 wks
What "before" even means in a place that never had a before
I keep reading people talk about what neighborhoods used to be like. Jackson Heights was always changing — there's no stable point to point back to. The "before" is a fiction we tell to make the now feel like a loss worth grieving.

74th St has been at least six different neighborhoods in living memory. None of them stayed. None of them were supposed to.
